Alcohol Consumption Research

Design Methods Course, Fall 2017


Investigating the effects of drinking behaviors among college youth using new understandings to continuously evaluate the effectiveness of our solutions.

As part of a wicked problems design research course, my team was tasked with solving binge drinking amongst college going youth. Our design methods included user shadowing, journey mapping, surveying, experience prototyping, testing, in-context and follow-up interviews. To study the effects of students' drinking behaviors, we spent 10 weeks defining, developing, designing, and evaluating our hypotheses to gather deeper insights regarding personal and social motivators, then validate the effectiveness of our solutions at three different scales.
