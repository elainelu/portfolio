Better World x Design

Organizing RISD & Brown's Annnual Interdisciplinary Design Conference

Throughout the year, I work with the organizing committee behind Better World by Design to plan the largest student-run sustainable design conference on RISD/Brown Campus.

Our mission is to celebrate interdisciplinary collaboration between designers, educators, innovators, technologists, and learners. Our year builds up to a 3-day conference on Brown and RISD’s campus, that reinvigorates these ideas and inspires us to continue these conversations. During the year, we facilitate workshops, panels, and discussions that engage in and promote design thinking.

Each year, 1000+ attendess and 70+ presenters fly in from around the world! This year's lineup include John Zelek, the Senior Creative at Soylent, Cynthia E. Smith, the Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum’s Curator of Socially Responsible Design, and the interdisciplinary artist, designer, and fellow at The Kennedy Center and Urban Design Forum, Ekene Ijeoma.




Theme Launch

Blocks, Bytes, and Bars, uses symbols to provide an alternative method of conceiving the design process. These three objects can represent design’s traditional past, technological present, and aspirational future; however, symbolism is inherently ambiguous and therefore invites multiple interpretations. We hope that crafting the theme in this way will provide a fresh lens through which new stories, understandings and connections can emerge.
