Better World x Design

Organizing RISD & Brown's Annnual Interdisciplinary Design Conference

Throughout the year, I work with the organizing committee behind Better World by Design to plan the largest student-run sustainable design conference in nation.

Our mission is to celebrate interdisciplinary collaboration between designers, educators, innovators, technologists, and learners. During the year, we facilitate workshops, panels, and discussions that engage in and promote design thinking.

Each year, 1000+ attendess and 70+ presenters fly in from around the world! This year's lineup include John Zelek, the Senior Creative at Soylent, Cynthia E. Smith, the Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum’s Curator of Socially Responsible Design, and the interdisciplinary artist, designer, and fellow at The Kennedy Center and Urban Design Forum, Ekene Ijeoma.

Theme Launch

Symbols provide an alternative method of conceiving the design process. These three objects can represent design’s traditional past, technological present, and aspirational future. Symbolism, however, is inherently ambiguous and invites multiple interpretations. We hope that crafting the theme in this way will provide a fresh lens through which new stories, understandings, and connections can emerge.


Volunteers

My team was in charge of recruiting volunteers and participant hosting. Applications were sent out during the last week of Summer. We selected 80 volunteers out of 100+ who applied, allocated roles, and assigned roles: Social Media, Operations, or Content.


Design Challenge

Here is a preview of our draft design challenge! This was created in partnership with ¡CityArts! for students and conference participants to construct a 5-week Wintersession course based on the following promp: How can we use design-thinking to engage youth in social justice topics? For our 10th anniversary conference, the Community Engagement team wanted to launch a more locally-based challenge. The winning submission will get prize money from our organization, and a more tangible teaching experience by engaging with youth in the Providence community. Better World x Design celebrates interdicipinary collaboration between designers, educators, and people from all disciplines that practice design thinking, and our team was interested in how arts education can facilitate such messages in our community.


Design Expo

My team was also responsible for organizing the Design Expo during our three-day September conference to showcase the ambitions and recent projects of local innovators, artists, entrepreneurs, technologists, and designers. The Expo took place on Saturday, September 23rd from 1:30-3:30 PM, when the entirety of Brown University’s Main Green will be open for innovators to show off their work: What it is, why it’s impactful, results, or anything else you deem relevant. As part of our exploration into design thinking, the Expo is one event in particular that aimed to broaden the horizons of our participants by showing them the different forms that design can take.
