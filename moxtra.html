Moxtra

Product Design Internship, Summer 2017

Moxtra is a cloud-based combination of group chat and document annotation, aiming to make projects easier to collaborate on across smartphones, tablets and computers.

Moxtra makes small teams more efficient. Developers can mix and match embeddables to build a completely customized collaboration experience. Users can connect with teams and individuals, then elevate the basic text-based chat to conversations with audio, video, and web clips, as well as share any file from their mobile device, desktop, or cloud storage services. All interactions and documentation are synchronized and archived.

Launch

During my time there, I worked on visual design details and animations, including animating svg graphics in CSS and Javascript. Initial microinteractions like this one add value to the user experience by helping introduce people to their brand. I desinged the new animated splash screen. For new users, this kicks off a walkthrough of the app's different functions. For returning users, it leads to the main group messaging screen.

Loading

The loading animation provides a first impression of the app, and helps introduce people to Moxtra's dashboard with motion. Animation also buys time to load data in the background while preserving an experience that feels quick and responsive. The new graphics were implemented to help visulize the main features and benefits.

I iterated by mixing and matching a compiliation of JS animations, before deciding to move forward with this one. The animation tests are documented in a little website that I hacked together. The documentation is available on GitHub.

Onboarding

I also worked to create new set of graphics displayed on the app's walkthrough. As the Moxtra iOS app stands, it continues the use of no shadows, highlights, and 1px icons in their applications. The clean transitions, combined with a few intentional animations makes the feel of the app not totally flat or overly embellished. Functionality preceeds fun interactions that would contribute more postively to UX in the context of competing group messaging and file sharing services.

The new set of graphics, as opposed to the app store appshots for Moxtra, are closer to the style of their UI element library. They are expecially closer to and stylistically inspired by Moxtra's own set of navigation icons!

Icons

Uploading media and text files is conveyed with just a few elements on the screen. The information is visually concise, with no added complexity while leading people to make the default decision to tap on an item and add files.

Most of their icons are simple, large rounded line drawings in black and white. You can also click "add more" on the bottom scroll bar to connect with addtional platforms such as Google Drive, Dropbox, and ever Evernote. These icons would then show up filled in with their specific logo colors, to maintain a distinction between local and external imports while orienting the user.

The pattern we have now come to expect, courtesy of messaging apps like iMessage and Messinger, is a split text entry bottom bar. Icons to upload media, images, and stickers are on the right, and the text bar is on the left. The Moxtra format dedicates a seperate navigation tab for full screen space to add files and folders. Think of it as a very simplifed google drive with a unique navigation heirarchy. The two "add file" buttons open a small dynamic bottom scroll bar. This takes over only part of the screen, maximizing the retainment of content for the primary file library space.
