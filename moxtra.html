Moxtra

Product Design Internship, Summer 2017

How can we strengthen Moxtra's visual language across across messaging on smartphones, tablets, and computers?

My role this summer was a cross between design research and product design! I experimented with motion graphics, ran geurilla tests, brainstormed a variety of solutions, wrote specs, defined goals, hypotheses, and success metrics. We redesigned the loading animations, microinteractions within the app, and led extensive A/B testing on their web app.

The product is mobile-first, but many of these mirroring web features had been neglected in terms of usability. The messaging portal is seperate from the developer portal, and has been For the last few years, it has been used mainly by developers for their open API/SDK. With this problem in mind, my journey began.

Moxtra users can elevate the basic text-based chat to conversations with audio, video, and web clips, as well as share any file from their mobile device, desktop, or cloud storage service.

Launch

I animated svg graphics like this one in CSS and Javascript. For new users, this kicks off a walkthrough of the app's different functions. For returning users, it leads to the group messaging home screen.

The loading animation adds value to UX by introducing people to the Moxtra brand. Animation also buys time to load data in the background while preserving an experience that feels quick and responsive. A new set of graphics were also made for the onboarding walkthrough, allowing people to imagine how they can use this app.

I iterated by mixing and matching a compiliation of JS animations, before deciding to move forward with this one. The animations are documented on this website that I put together. See documentation on GitHub.

Onboarding

I also helped make a new set of graphics for the app's walkthrough. These screens are stylistically inspired by Moxtra's own set of navigation icons! As the app stands, it continues the use of no shadows, 1px icons, and 1 second bounce animations. The clean transitions and illustrations make the app feel not totally flat or complex despite its complex integration functionalities.

Icons

(Right) Uploading media and text files is conveyed with just a few elements on the screen. The information is visually concise, with no added complexity while leading people to make the default decision to tap on an item and add files.

(Left) Most of their icons are simple, rounded line drawings. These were made by referencing a combination of their current mobile, desktop, and brand ID icons. Click "add more" on the bottom scroll bar to connect with platforms such as Google Drive, Dropbox, and ever Evernote. The Moxtra format dedicates a seperate navigation tab for full screen space to add files and folders. The dynamic bottom scroll bar takes over a small part of the screen, maximizing the retainment of content for the primary file library space.
