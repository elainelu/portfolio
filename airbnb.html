Airbnb UX Redesign

KBCP Design Challenge, January 2018


This UX redesign was completed as part of the Kleiner Perkins Fellowship application. Selected as a finalist in 2018.

This design sprint was completed in one week. During the first few days, I spoke with, interviewed, and surveyed 15 Airbnb customers in order to hypothesize improvements for the website experience. Common concerns that came up in conversation helped me imagine what the typical Airbnb customer looks like, why they use Airbnb, what keeps them coming back and what priorities are top of mind when traveling. I interviewed 9 and 6 males ranging from 20-48 years old. The responses can be found here.

Homes, Experiences, Restaurants

I looked to the following three primary sections of the website when considering ways to improve usability: homes, experiences, and restaurants. The Airbnb website functions as a store but there is ample opportunity for the feed and individual listings to work together to faciliatate a more cohesive browsing experience. The site is content- and filter-saturated, there is a lack of associative entry points from home feed to listings pages to Guidebooks and Wish Lists, and the grid layout could more effectively encourage integrated browsing between experiences and restaurants.

What are the opportunity areas?

Opportunity 1: Personalizing the browsing experience. Airbnb provides experiences people love that the website could reflect. Currently, it does not enable people to make discoveries through browsing. There is an opportunity for personalized suggestions that allow people to be inspired by the places they could go and experiences they can have for low prices.

Opportunity 2: Building your Wish List. 11/15 people who I surveyed do not use Wish Lists regularly and 4/15 have never seen the Wish Lists page. This feature is important for allowing customers to discover and bring their travel ideas to life. As such, Wish Lists and Guidebooks have the potential to establish and build networks of people who have similar travel preferences.

Opportunity 3: People do not visit the Airbnb website unless they have a destination in mind. The listings on the website are meant to be transactional. Photos show what accomodations look like but not what they feel like. Potential improvements inlude larger photos, multimedia, videos, and highlights that turn pages into an interactive space. This way, so people can have a better a idea of what it feel like to be there in person.



Next Steps: Airbnb Spaces

What does it mean to be inclusive? Airbnb operates in neighborhoods, and the next step is to operate in communities. There is currently no centralized platform for people to find available open spaces for co-working or hosting activites that feel intimate and comfortable. People use Airbnb to find a place to stay, but everyone should be able to easily book nice spaces for hosting.

Airbnb would gain profits from individual event organizer subscriptions and from selling package deals to local businesses and companies who wish to sponsor their events at local or global conference centers. The eventual goal is to allow hosts and event organizers of any scale to rent out available location properties to host, organize, and promote social activites.
