Amazon Alexa

UX Design Internship, Summer 2018


I spent summer 2018 interning on the Alexa communications design team in Seattle.

I spent 12 weeks working in the Devices and Services Design Group, devising new voice-forward features for Alexa communications on Echo devices. The communications domain inclues calling, messaging, drop in, contacts and announcements. My project was to frame and propose new features for communications that will increase engagement through delightful customer experiences. I navigated the full UX design cycle from documenting use cases and writing product priorities, to wireframing, prototyping, and testing.

By the end of the summer, I designed a new voice-forward social messaging experience. This feature enables customers to more easily discover contacts via Alexa. It also combines Alexa communications with Amazon's information domain, while building on the existing architecture for messaging. I handed off the project in my final week and had 6 formal presentations for our VP of Design, Senior PMs, developers, UX Researchers and Design Directors to continue its lifecyle.

Brief: Create a delightful home screen experience to raise awareness and increase engagement for comms.

"The home screen for Echo devices is a place that presents lightweight, glanceable information that may be relevant to the customer. Currently, the communications team has only a single 'presence' card on screened Echo devices that is tightly integrated with Drop in."

I was tasked to define UX concepts that will drive customer engagement, while creating delightful moments for comms on home panels of screened devices.
